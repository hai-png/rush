## 3. Obligations of the Processor
The Processor agrees to:
a) Process personal data only on documented instructions from the Controller
b) Ensure personnel are subject to confidentiality obligations
c) Implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures
d) Notify the Controller of any personal data breach within 48 hours
e) Assist the Controller in fulfilling data subject rights requests
f) Delete or return personal data at the Controller's request
g) Maintain records of all processing activities under this agreement

## 4. Security Measures
The Processor shall maintain:
- Encryption at rest and in transit
- Access controls (least privilege principle)
- Regular security testing and vulnerability assessments
- Incident response procedures
- Business continuity and disaster recovery plans

## 5. Sub-processing
The Processor may engage sub-processors only with the Controller's prior written authorization. The Processor shall remain fully liable for sub-processor compliance.

## 6. Data Breach Notification
The Processor shall notify the Controller within 48 hours of becoming aware of any personal data breach affecting Controller data, including:
- Nature of the breach
- Categories and approximate number of data subjects affected
- Categories and approximate volume of personal data records affected
- Remedial measures taken or proposed

## 7. Compliance with Proclamation 1321/2024
Both parties agree to process personal data in compliance with Ethiopia's Data Protection Proclamation 1321/2024, including:
- Article 12-14 (lawful processing bases)
- Article 21 (records of processing activities)
- Article 24-25 (data subject rights)
- Article 27 (data protection impact assessments)
- Article 30 (breach notification)

## 8. Governing Law
This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the Federal Democratic Republic of Ethiopia.

## 9. Audit Rights (FIX COMPL-004)

The Controller has the right, at its own expense and no more than once per calendar year, to audit the Processor's compliance with this DPA. The audit shall:

a) Be conducted with at least 30 days' written notice, except in the case of a suspected breach or regulatory investigation where the Controller may request an immediate audit.
b) Be conducted during the Processor's normal business hours and in a manner that minimizes disruption to the Processor's operations.
c) Be performed by the Controller's internal audit team or by an independent third-party auditor bound by confidentiality obligations no less stringent than those in this DPA.
d) Cover the Processor's technical and organizational security measures, records of processing activities, sub-processor agreements, and breach response procedures.
e) Be limited to information relevant to the Controller's data — the Processor may redact information unrelated to the Controller or that is subject to legal privilege.

The Processor shall provide reasonable cooperation and access to facilities, systems, and personnel necessary for the audit. The Controller shall document any findings in a written report shared with the Processor. The Processor shall remediate any material findings within 60 days of the report, or such shorter period as may be required by law or by the severity of the finding.

If the Processor undergoes an industry-standard independent audit (e.g., SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001) that covers the same scope as the Controller's audit right, the Processor may share the resulting report with the Controller in lieu of a separate audit, subject to the Controller's reasonable acceptance of the report's scope.

## 11. Cross-Border Data Transfers (FIX COMPL-004)

For any transfer of personal data outside the Federal Democratic Republic of Ethiopia, the Processor shall implement appropriate safeguards as required by Proclamation 1321/2024 Art. 19 and any implementing regulations. Such safeguards may include:

a) **Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs):** For transfers to processors in jurisdictions without an adequacy decision, the parties shall execute the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses (Decision 2021/914) or any successor SCCs adopted under Ethiopian law, incorporated herein by reference.

b) **Adequacy Decision:** Where the recipient jurisdiction has been recognized by the Ethiopian Data Protection Authority (or the competent supervisory authority under Proclamation 1321/2024) as providing an adequate level of personal data protection, no further safeguards are required beyond this DPA.

c) **Binding Corporate Rules:** For intra-group transfers, the Processor may rely on Binding Corporate Rules approved by the competent supervisory authority.

d) **Transfer Impact Assessment:** Before any new cross-border transfer, the Processor shall conduct a transfer impact assessment considering the destination jurisdiction's surveillance laws, government access practices, and the availability of effective remedies for data subjects. The assessment shall be documented and provided to the Controller upon request.

e) **Supplementary Measures:** Where the destination jurisdiction's laws undermine the effectiveness of the above safeguards, the Processor shall implement supplementary measures (e.g., strong encryption with Controller-held keys, pseudonymization, split processing) such that the personal data is effectively protected.

The Processor shall notify the Controller within 30 days of any change in the legal framework of a destination jurisdiction that materially affects the safeguards relied upon.

## 12. Term and Termination (FIX COMPL-004)

This DPA shall remain in effect for the duration of the Controller's use of the Processor's services and thereafter as necessary to fulfill the obligations in this DPA regarding the return or deletion of personal data.

**Termination by the Controller:** The Controller may terminate this DPA:

a) Immediately upon written notice if the Processor commits a material breach of this DPA that the Controller cannot reasonably remedy, including unauthorized disclosure of personal data, failure to maintain required security measures, or engagement of an unauthorized sub-processor.
b) With 30 days' written notice if the Controller objects to a new sub-processor under Section 10 and the parties cannot reach agreement on alternative arrangements.
c) With 90 days' written notice for convenience.

**Termination by the Processor:** The Processor may terminate this DPA with 90 days' written notice if continued processing would place the Processor in violation of applicable law, provided the Processor gives the Controller a reasonable opportunity to migrate to an alternative processor.

**Effect of Termination:** Upon termination, the Processor shall, at the Controller's choice:

a) Return all personal data to the Controller in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format (e.g., JSON, CSV); AND
b) Delete all remaining copies of the personal data from the Processor's production systems within 30 days; AND
c) Certify the deletion in writing, signed by an authorized officer of the Processor.

The Processor may retain personal data only to the extent required by applicable law (e.g., tax retention obligations), in which case the Processor shall inform the Controller of the legal basis, the categories of data retained, the retention period, and the measures taken to prevent further processing for other purposes.
